Beverly Hills Estate — Antique & Fine Art Relocation

Relocating irreplaceable antiques and fine art from a Beverly Hills estate to a new home in Santa Barbara, with white-glove care at every step.

Project Overview

When Every Piece Is Irreplaceable

A longtime Beverly Hills collector needed to relocate a lifetime of antique furniture, fine art, and a centerpiece 12-seater antique dining table to their new Santa Barbara residence. With pieces dating back to the 18th century and a combined collection value in the hundreds of thousands, this was not a standard move — it was a preservation operation.

Elite Movers assigned a dedicated white-glove crew of six specialists. Every piece was individually assessed, photographed, and given a custom wrapping plan. The antique table was disassembled by hand — no power tools — with each leg, leaf, and joinery point padded in acid-free tissue and triple-wrapped in moving blankets. Framed artwork received corner protectors, glassine barriers, and custom-fitted mirror boxes.

Floor runners were laid throughout both the Beverly Hills estate and the Santa Barbara destination to prevent any contact between equipment and the original hardwood and marble floors. The entire operation was completed over two days with climate-controlled transport between locations.

From the Move

The Challenge

Centuries of History on the Line

The 12-seater antique dining table was the single most challenging piece. It weighed over 400 pounds, featured hand-carved legs with delicate scrollwork, and could not be tilted beyond 15 degrees without risking joint separation. It had to navigate through a doorway with less than two inches of clearance on either side.

Several of the oil paintings required specific humidity and temperature conditions during transport. The client also required a detailed photographic inventory of every item before and after the move, with condition notes for insurance purposes. Nothing could be rushed — and nothing could be replaced.

The Result

Not a Scratch, Not a Worry

Every item arrived at the Santa Barbara residence in the exact condition it left Beverly Hills. The antique table was reassembled in the new dining room without a single mark on the original finish. All artwork was hung according to the client's layout plan, and the photographic inventory confirmed zero damage across the entire collection.

Floor protection was removed and both properties were left spotless. The client's insurer confirmed the move met museum-grade handling standards.
